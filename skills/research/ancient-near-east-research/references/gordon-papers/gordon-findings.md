Gordon's work establishes crucial links between Minoan Crete, Ugarit, and Biblical traditions.

## Key Findings

### 1. The Idaean Footstool (Ugarit and Caphtor, p. 127)

The Ugaritic craftsman god Kothar-wa-Khasis fashions furniture referencing Mount Ida:

> **hdm id** (Ugaritic text 51:1:35) = "a footstool of Ida"

**Linguistic chain:**
- Ugaritic **hdm** → Hebrew **הֲדֹם** (hadom) = "footstool"
- Also attested in Egyptian as **hdmw** (loanword from Caphtorian)

**Biblical occurrences of hadom:**
- Isaiah 66:1 — "the earth is my footstool"
- Psalm 110:1 — "until I make your enemies your footstool"
- Lamentations 2:1 — "remembered not his footstool"

This establishes Mount Ida (sacred Cretan mountain) appearing directly in Ugaritic literature.

**Where Ida comes up (p. 127):**
> "The Cretan affinities of «Caphtor» are borne out by the description of Ktr-and-Hss at work in his atelier, where he fashions an Idaean footstool: hdm id (text 51:1:35) «a footstool of Ida.»"

Gordon also notes:
> "In all probability id refers to Mount Ida in Crete, though we should note that «Ida» is also found in Asia Minor; cf. the Mount Ida overlooking Troy (mentioned often in the Iliad). Indeed it is likely that the name «Ida» was brought to Crete by immigrants from Asia Minor."

**Important distinction:** The hdm id (footstool of Ida) is made by **Kothar-wa-Khasis** (the Ugaritic craftsman god), NOT by Bezalel. Bezalel's Caphtorian connection is through the **kaftôrîm** (decorative knobs on the Menorah), not through Ida/footstool imagery.

### 2. Moses-Minos Pattern (Ugarit and Caphtor, p. 131)

Gordon identifies the divine law-giver archetype:

> "The pattern of Minos as the giver of divine law (obtained according to some traditions on a mountain) has been followed by Moses, who got the Law from God on Mount Sinai, to lay down therewith the foundations of society."

| Minoan Pattern | Biblical Parallel |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Minos | Moses |
| Receives law on mountain | Receives Torah on Sinai |
| Divine sanction for legislation | Divine sanction for legislation |
| Founds Cretan society | Founds Israelite society |

### 3. Daedalus-Bezalel Pattern (Ugarit and Caphtor, p. 131)

The master-craftsman parallel:

> "The second greatest figure in Minoan tradition, namely Daedalus, has his counterpart in Moses's master-craftsman Bezalel. And to dispel any doubt... the creations of Bezalel include **kaftôrîm** «caphtors», bearing the name of the homeland of early Israel's major source of cultural inspiration."

| Minoan Pattern | Biblical Parallel |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Daedalus | Bezalel (בְּצַלְאֵל) |
| Master craftsman | Master craftsman (Exodus 31:1-5) |
| Creates automata (Talos) | Creates Tabernacle furnishings |
| Divine inspiration | Filled with "Spirit of Elohim" |

**The kaftôrîm (כַּפְתֹּרִים):**
- Decorative knobs/capitals on the Menorah (Exodus 25:31-36, 37:17-22)
- Name derives from **Caphtor** (כַּפְתּוֹר) = Crete
- Bezalel's work explicitly references the Minoan homeland

### 4. Caphtor = Crete

Gordon confirms the identification:
- Hebrew **כַּפְתּוֹר** (Kaphtor) = Crete
- Amos 9:7 — "Did I not bring... the Philistines from Caphtor?"
- Jeremiah 47:4 — "the remnant of the coastland of Caphtor"
- Deuteronomy 2:23 — "the Caphtorim, who came from Caphtor"

## Gordon's Methodology

Gordon approached Linear A and Eteocretan as **Northwest Semitic languages**, arguing:

1. Minoan Crete had significant Semitic-speaking populations
2. Cultural exchange between Ugarit and Crete was extensive
3. Biblical traditions preserve Minoan patterns filtered through Canaanite intermediaries

This challenges the conventional view of isolated cultural development and supports Mediterranean-wide religious and artistic transmission.
