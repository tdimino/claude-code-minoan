# Minoan Grammar — Ugaritic Model

Morphology and syntax of reconstructed Minoan, modeled on Ugaritic (the closest fully-documented NWS language) and calibrated by Linear A morphological evidence.

## Word Order

**VSO** (verb–subject–object) is the default, standard for NWS:
> *yatana Kupānu yēna la-Atiratu*
> "Kupanu gave wine to Athirat"

**SVO** for topicalization (fronting the subject for emphasis):
> *Kupānu yatana yēna la-Atiratu*
> "KUPANU (he's the one who) gave wine to Athirat"

**Verbless clauses** (nominal sentences) for predication:
> *Atiratu rabbatu*
> "Athirat is great" (lit. "Athirat — great-one")

## The Verbal System

### Root System
Triconsonantal roots (standard Semitic). An archaic biconsonantal layer persists, expanded by nun-determinatives and other extensions (see Thera-Knossos research: `biconsonantal-roots-nun-determinative.md`).

### Stems

| Stem | Pattern | Function | Example (√Y-T-N) |
|------|---------|----------|-------------------|
| **G** (basic) | qatala / yaqtulu | Simple active | yatana "he gave" |
| **D** (intensive) | qattala / yuqattilu | Intensive/factitive | yattana "he gave abundantly" |
| **N** (passive) | naqtala / yinqatilu | Simple passive | nayatana "it was given" |
| **Š** (causative) | šaqtala / yušaqtilu | Causative | šaytana "he caused to give" |
| **Gt** (reflexive) | qitatala | Reflexive of G | — |
| **Dt** (reflexive) | qutatila | Reflexive of D | — |

The G-stem and D-stem are attested in Linear A morphology. N-stem and Š-stem are inferred from NWS parallels.

### G-Stem Paradigm: Y-T-N "to give"

#### Perfective (suffix conjugation — completed action)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 3ms | yatana | yatanū |
| 3fs | yatanat | yatanā |
| 2ms | yatanta | yatantum |
| 2fs | yatanti | yatantinna |
| 1cs | yatantu | yatannū |

#### Imperfective (prefix conjugation — ongoing/future action)

| Person | Singular | Plural |
|--------|----------|--------|
| 3ms | yatanu | yatanūna |
| 3fs | tatanu | tatanūna |
| 2ms | tatanu | tatanūna |
| 2fs | tatanīna | tatanūna |
| 1cs | atanu | natanu |

**Prefix alternation**: Both a- and ya- attested in Linear A for 3ms (A-TA-NO / JA-TA-NO). This may reflect dialectal variation or an archaic feature where the *yaqtul* prefix had not yet fully regularized.

## The Nominal System

### Gender
- **Masculine**: unmarked (zero ending + case vowel)
- **Feminine**: **-tu** / **-atu** suffix

Attested in Linear A:
- KU-PA-NU (m.) → KU-PA-NA-TU (f.) — the -TU feminine marker
- PA-DA-SU (m.) → PA-DA-SU-TI (f.) — the -TI feminine marker
- DA-KU-SE-NE (m.) → DA-KU-SE-NE-TI (f.)

### Number

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|----------|----------|
| Singular | -u / -a / -i | -tu / -atu |
| Dual | -āmi | -atāmi |
| Plural | -ūma (nom) / -īma (obl) | -ātu |

Attested plural marker: NO-X-NA-MA (sg.) → NO-X-NA-MI-NA (pl.) — the -MI-NA ending corresponds to masculine plural -īma + demonstrative -na.

### Case Vowels

| Case | Vowel | Function |
|------|-------|----------|
| Nominative | -u | Subject |
| Accusative | -a | Direct object |
| Genitive | -i | Possession, after prepositions |

Case vowels are written in Linear A as part of the final CV sign but may be "dead vowels" in some positions. Like Ugaritic, case distinction is functional in formal/literary registers and may have weakened in colloquial speech.

### States

| State | Form | Use |
|-------|------|-----|
| **Absolute** | full form + case | Independent noun |
| **Construct** | shortened (drops case) | "X of Y" (genitive chain) |

Construct chains:
- *bēt Atirati* "house of Athirat" (bētu loses -u in construct)
- *sipinatu Kupāni* "ship of Kupanu"
- *yēn Kaphturi* "wine of Kaphtor"

### No Definite Article

Ugaritic has no definite article. Minoan follows this pattern. Definiteness is expressed by:
- Context (as in Ugaritic prose)
- Demonstrative suffix **-na** ("this"): *manatu-na* = "this offering"
- Proper nouns are inherently definite

## Particles and Prepositions

### Conjunctions

| Particle | Meaning | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **u** | and | Gordon 1962: Minoan uses *u* (not *wa*) |
| **ki** | so that, because | Attested: KI in Linear A |
| **ʾap** | also, even | Ugaritic *ap* |
| **pan** | lest | Ugaritic *pn* |

### Prepositions

| Particle | Meaning | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **la-** / **le-** | to, for | Attested: RE in Linear A (Gordon reads as /le/) |
| **bi-** | in, with, by | Standard NWS |
| **min** | from, out of | Standard NWS |
| **ʿal** | upon, over | Ugaritic *ʿl* |
| **taḥt** | under, beneath | Ugaritic *tḥt* |
| **bayn** | between | Ugaritic *bn* (preposition) |

### Demonstratives

| Form | Meaning | Notes |
|------|---------|-------|
| **-na** (suffix) | this | Attested: -MA-NA / -MI-NA in Linear A |
| **hanna** / **hannā** | this (independent) | Ugaritic model |

### Interrogatives

| Form | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **mā** | what? |
| **mannu** | who? |
| **ʾayna** | where? |
| **matay** | when? |
| **kī** | how? |

### The -AN Onomastic Suffix

Attested on multiple names: MI-NA-NE (Minos + -ān), KI-RE-TA-NA (Kret + -ān), DA-NA-NE (Dan + -ān), KA-DU-MA-NE (Cadmos + -ān). This is a NWS hypocoristic/gentilic suffix: "one of X," "belonging to X."

## Negation

| Particle | Use | Example |
|----------|-----|---------|
| **lā** | indicative negation | lā yatana "he did not give" |
| **ʾal** | prohibitive (jussive) | ʾal tatanu "do not give!" |
| **bal** | absolute negation | bal ilūma "there are no gods" (oath formula) |

## Relative Clauses

Relative pronoun **du** (attested as DU in Linear A):
> *sipinatu du yatana Kupānu*
> "the ship that Kupanu gave"

## Sentence Patterns

### Declarative
> *yatana Kupānu yēna la-Atiratu*
> "Kupanu gave wine to Athirat" (VSO)

### Purpose Clause (ki + imperfective)
> *yasharamu ki-tattibu Kiriyatu*
> "a righteous offering so that the City may thrive"

### Nominal (verbless)
> *Atiratu rabbatu Yammi*
> "Athirat is the Great Lady of the Sea"

### Imperative
> *tun yēna la-Addu!*
> "Give wine to Addu!"

### Votive Formula
> *tanuʾati suparāna la-Addu — Dawida*
> "I set up this pot for Addu — Dawida"
