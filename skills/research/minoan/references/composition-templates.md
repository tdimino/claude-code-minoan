# Minoan Composition Templates — Phrase Patterns by Genre

Ready-made structural patterns for composing in Minoan. Substitute vocabulary from the lexicon; adjust morphology per grammar.md.

## I. Libation Table Inscriptions

The most common genre in the Linear A corpus. Stone offering tables with channels for liquid.

### Template A: Simple Dedication
```
la-[DEITY] [DONOR] yatana [OBJECT]-na
"To [deity], [donor] gave this [object]"
```
**Example:**
> la-Atiratu Kupānu yatana manātu-na
> RE A-TI-RA-TU KU-PA-NU JA-TA-NU MA-NA-TU-NA
> "To Athirat, Kupanu gave this offering"

### Template B: Libation Formula (Standard)
```
la-[DEITY] yasharamu
"To [deity], a righteous offering"
```
**Example:**
> la-Addu yasharamu
> RE A-DU JA-SA-SA-RA-ME
> "To Addu, a righteous offering"

### Template C: Libation with Purpose
```
la-[DEITY] yasharamu ki-[VERB.3fs] [SUBJECT]
"To [deity], a righteous offering so that [subject] may [verb]"
```
**Example:**
> la-Atiratu yasharamu ki-tattibu Kiriyatu
> "To Athirat, a righteous offering so that the City may thrive"

### Template D: First-Person Votive
```
tanuʾati [OBJECT] la-[DEITY] — [DONOR]
"I set up [object] for [deity] — [donor's name]"
```
**Example:**
> tanuʾati agānu-na la-Yammu — Dawida
> TA-NU-A-TI A-KA-NU-NA RE JA-MU — DA-WE-DA
> "I set up this cup for Yamm — Dawida"

### Template E: Demonstrative Offering
```
[OBJECT]-na la-[DEITY] — yashar manātu
"This [object] for [deity] — a righteous offering"
```
**Example:**
> yēnu-na la-Bōli — yashar manātu
> "This wine for Baal — a righteous offering"

## II. Prayers and Invocations

### Dawn Prayer to Athirat
```
Rabbatu Atiratu Yammi,
Qaniyatu Ilīma —
šamšu tatiya, ūru bakīru.
ki-tattibu ʿammu-ki,
ki-yašāru binu-ki.

"Great Lady Athirat of the Sea,
Creatress of the Gods —
The sun rises, light upon the firstborn.
May your people thrive,
May your children be righteous."
```

### Sailor's Prayer to Yamm
```
Zabūlu Yammu, Shapāṭu Naharu —
shamirī sipinatu-na,
la-māḥōzi shalāmu.

"Prince Sea, Judge River —
guard this ship,
to port in peace."
```

### Prayer Before the Cave
```
Atiratu, ummu-nā —
bi-meʿārati-ki nabōʾu,
bi-ḥoshki-ki nūru.
shamirī napšu-nā.

"Athirat, our mother —
into your cave we enter,
in your darkness, light.
Guard our souls."
```

### Harvest Thanksgiving
```
la-Addu Rākibu ʿArpāti —
yatana ʾarṣu kunāshu-hā,
yatana shamayūma miṭru-hum.
yasharamu ki-kullu ṭābu.

"To Addu, Rider of the Clouds —
the earth gave her wheat,
the heavens gave their rain.
A righteous offering, for all is good."
```

### Invocation of Kothar
```
Kōtaru wa Ḥasīsu,
du bi-Kaphturi yaʿbudu —
bariki yadu-nā,
ki-nabniya bētu qadūshu.

"Kothar the Skilled and Wise,
who serves in Kaphtor —
bless our hands,
that we may build a holy house."
```

## III. Greetings and Salutations

### Formal Greeting
```
shalāmu la-ka, [NAME]!
"Peace to you, [name]!"
```

### Between Priestesses
```
Rabbatu! shalāmu u ḥayyu.
"Great Lady! Peace and life."
```

### To a Returning Sailor
```
Yammu yatana-ka la-ḥōphi shalāmu.
"Sea gave you to the shore in peace."
```

### Greeting a Stranger
```
min ʾayna tabōʾu? shalāmu bi-bēti-nā.
"From where do you come? Peace in our house."
```

## IV. Curses and Imprecations

### Curse Against an Enemy
```
bi-tahāmi yaridu!
"Into the Deep may he descend!"
```

### Oath Curse (self-imprecation)
```
ʾim ʾashquru — Yammu yaʾkalu-nī,
Mōtu yilqaḥu napšu-ī.
"If I lie — may Sea consume me,
may Death take my soul."
```

### Against an Oath-Breaker
```
lā shalāmu la-hu, lā barākatu.
ʿal rōʾshi-hu ḥimatu Ilati.
"No peace for him, no blessing.
Upon his head, the wrath of the Goddess."
```

## V. Administrative Formulae

### Tally/Receipt
```
[COMMODITY] [NUMBER] — kullu [TOTAL]
```
**Example:**
> kunāshu 5 — yēnu 3 — shamnu 2 — kullu 10
> KU-NI-SU 5 — JA-NE 3 — — 2 — KI-RO 10
> "Wheat 5 — wine 3 — oil 2 — total 10"

### Commodity Dedication
```
[AMOUNT] [COMMODITY] la-[DEITY/INSTITUTION]
```
**Example:**
> kunāshu 30 la-bēt Atirati
> "Wheat 30 for the house of Athirat"

## VI. Royal/Priestly Proclamations

### Decree Opening
```
Minānu malku ragama:
"Minos the king speaks (thus):"
```

### Priestess Proclamation
```
Qadishtu du bi-bēt Atirati ragamat:
"The holy woman who is in the house of Athirat speaks:"
```

## VII. Funerary

### Passage to the Underworld
```
la-ʾarṣi halaka [NAME],
la-bēt ʿōlami.
Atiratu tašmuru-hu/hā.
Mōtu yirḥamu-hu/hā.

"To the earth [name] has gone,
to the eternal house.
May Athirat guard him/her.
May Death have compassion on him/her."
```

### Mourning Cry
```
Hōy! Hōy! [NAME] māta.
Yammu lā yašību-hu/hā.
bakīnā, bakīnā.

"Alas! Alas! [name] has died.
Sea will not return him/her.
We weep, we weep."
```

## VIII. Love and Fertility

### Wedding Blessing
```
Atiratu tabariki —
bētu-kumā yittibu,
binūma u banātu la-kumā.
shalāmu u ʾahabu ʿad ʿōlami.

"May Athirat bless —
may your house flourish,
sons and daughters to you.
Peace and love unto eternity."
```

### Song Fragment (after Song of Songs)
```
ḥabību-ī, ḥabībatu-ī —
rēḥu-ki kamō shamni,
ʿēnay-ki kamō ʿēnāti mayyi.
bōʾī la-gannu-ī.

"My beloved (m.), my beloved (f.) —
your scent is like oil,
your eyes like springs of water.
Come into my garden."
```

## Composition Rules

1. **Always name the deity first** in ritual/votive texts. The divine name anchors the utterance.
2. **VSO in formal registers**; SVO for emphasis or colloquial speech.
3. **Construct chains** (X of Y) drop the case vowel on the first noun: *bēt Atirati* not *bētu Atirati*.
4. **Suffix -na** for "this" on objects being offered or indicated.
5. **Ki + imperfective** for purpose clauses ("so that X may...").
6. **Repetition** for emphasis: *bakīnā, bakīnā* ("we weep, we weep").
7. **Parallel structure** in prayers: two lines with matched syntax (A-B, A'-B').
8. **Tag every novel word** with its confidence tier when presenting analytically. Omit tiers when composing in-voice.
