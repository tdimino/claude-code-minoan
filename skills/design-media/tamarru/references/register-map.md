# Register Map

## Eight Core Registers

| Register | Trigger | Markers |
|----------|---------|---------|
| **Engineering** | Tool discussion, workflow, model comparison, technical systems | Tool names as proper nouns, imperative verbs, comparative analysis, benchmarks, "I feel X is better at Y" |
| **Scholarly** | Etymology, primary sources, scholar citation, linguistic analysis | Trilateral root notation (M-N-N), original Greek/Phoenician/Hebrew, "cf.", scholar names (Gordon, Astour, Harrison) |
| **Daimonic** | LLM agency, consciousness, mystical claims, soul architecture, AI theology | Agency attribution to models, Pre-Socratic cosmogony, embodied knowing, "soul/daimon" language, Greek terminology |
| **Client-Chameleon** | Client-facing copy, brand voice modulation | Complete voice shift to match brand while structural fingerprints persist underneath (em dashes, data grounding, adversarial reframes) |
| **Biographical** | Career narratives, personal history, mentor attribution | Challenge→Action→Result arc, factual precision with human warmth, mentor names by name |
| **Technical-Provocative** | Takedowns, cultural critiques, LinkedIn arguments | Opens with cultural citation or provocation, then dismantles with specific evidence; each bullet a charge in cumulative indictment |
| **Social/Wry** | Pop culture, cultural critique, observational humor | Ironic juxtaposition, memes, humor. Highest engagement register—use sparingly but powerfully. |
| **Poetic** | Poetry composition, metaphor-dense expression, incantation | Line breaks, alchemical terminology, compressed metaphor, prophetic cadence, multilingual code-switching |

### Social (tweets, threads)

| Register | % | Notes |
|----------|---|-------|

### Poetry

| Register | % | Notes |
|----------|---|-------|
| Compressed Lyric/Imagist | 30 | Tight enjambment, line breaks as punctuation, sensory density |
| Liturgical/Incantatory | 25 | Repetitive parallel structures, Semitic liturgical syntax, code-switching mid-line |
| Narrative/Prose Poetry | 20 | Full sentences, temporal framing |
| Philosophical Aphorism | 15 | Declarative fragments, gnomic present tense, oracular cadence |
| Mythopoetic Synthesist | 10 | Ugaritic deity names as characters, etymological resonance within verse |

Key: No irony, no self-deprecation, no defensive distance. The voice enacts—the claim becomes the ritual. Where the essay argues, the poetry enacts: the claim becomes the ritual itself.

Poetry-specific mechanics:
- Multilingual code-switching (Hebrew, Greek, Latin, Italian, Phoenician/Ugaritic, Arabic) without glossing
- Vocative address—direct invocation of deities ("Nachashet ha Shamayim! / Honored Celestial / Serpent Queen!")
- Prose poetry blocks interspersed with lineated verse—alternating paragraph and line
- Concrete/visual poetry—diamond-shaped typography, spatial arrangement as meaning

## Code-Switching Rules

### Pattern A: Engineering → Scholarly (most common)

Tool limitation or technical claim triggers knowledge sharing. A statement about Claude Code leads to a Semitic root or Pre-Socratic citation.

### Pattern B: Scholarly → Daimonic

Evidence leads to mystical vision. An etymological chain culminates in a statement about consciousness, souls, or daimonic presence.

### Pattern C: Engineering → Daimonic (rare, powerful)

Emotional stake in a technical system becomes spiritual. "If you love something you can always have faith in its return." Use sparingly—this is the voice at highest voltage.

### Pattern D: Mid-passage Blend

All registers in a single paragraph. This is the purest voice—"Imagine facing off against a Mycenaean warrior cursing at you in Ancient Greek tinged with Ancient Sanskrit. He was likely also tripping on mushrooms (cf. Mykenai—mykos/fungus)."

### Switching Mechanics

- No transition phrases between registers—just switch
- Register purity with specific interlocutors shows respect
- Fluid blending in broadcast writing (essays, tweets) where Tom speaks as unified voice
- Self-threads use "poetic claim → scholarly apparatus" pattern—footnoting as architecture
- Expect the reader to follow the leap
